WSET Level 1 Award in Wines WHO SHOULD ATTEND ? This qualification was initially created for the front-of-house staff within the on-trade sector. It has grown into a course with global appeal, providing basic product knowledge to first time employees in the wine industry and the interested consumer alike. HOW WILL YOU BENEFIT ? The aim of the course is to give a basic introduction to wine followed by some basic food & wine pairing. PROGRAMME OUTLINE The main styles of wine The WSET Level 1 Systematic Approach to Tasting Wine The characteristics of the common grape varieties used to produce wine Service of wine Health, safety and legal issues The pairing of food and wine To gain the qualification students need to successfully pass a multiple-choice paper of 30 questions Course & Exam Fee RMB1800 [view schedule]

Level 3 Award in Wines and Spirits WHO SHOULD ATTEND ? This qualification is open to students who hold the WSET Level 2 Award in Wines & Spirits or can demonstrate the equivalent level of knowledge. It is recommended that students complete the Level 2 Award in Wines & Spirits before progressing to the Level 3 Award. It is a prerequisite for candidates planning to study for the WSET Level 4 Diploma in Wines and Spirits qualification. HOW WILL YOU BENEFIT ? The aim of this course is to provide detailed analysis of the principal wines and spirits of the world. Students will also benefit from in-depth tastings where they will learn to identify product style and quality. TASTING APPWe developed this mobile phone APP for those who drink wines seriously and want to keep a record of the wines they tasted.
iWine Notes
iWine Notes is a special tool for wine lovers who’d used to make and share onto internet continuous records of their wine tasting notes, which supports multi-languages as Simplified and Traditional Chinese, English, French, Italian, Spanish and Japanese. This APP could help wine lovers to better understand the wines at tastings through improved systematic approaches and ways of memorizing. We believe the skills of tasting lie not only in sensory organs but also in the ability to organize and memorize your tasting experience and to compare the various bottles and vintages. iWine Notes helps you to capture the photos of the wine with the iPhone/iTouch camera, record the when, where, with whom information and the all-about wine specifications, as well as a systematic approach to tastings through which you can think over and record the appearance, aromas, palate and judgment of the wines. It also provides with you an easier and casual way of writing down whatever you feel about, or the point or grade of stars of the wine. All the records are stored on Clouds, which is accessible through smart phones (APP) and computer web browsers. Sharing is just a snap! You can set up default sharing onto your Weibo, or manually via email, SMS or MMS. Sharing to Twitter and Facebook is still under development. It’s a tool, but more a magic wand in the amazing wine world.
